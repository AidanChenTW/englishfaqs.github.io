## [wind(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/wind1_1)

1. (also **the wind**) air that moves quickly as a result of natural forces
   - A gust of wind blew my hat off.
2. (British English, North American English **gas**, uncountable) air that you swallow with food or drind; gas that is produced in your stomach or intestines that makes you feel uncomfortable
   - Try to bring the baby's wind up.
3. [uncountable] breath that you need when you do exercise or blow into a musical instrument
   - He kicked Gomez in the stomach, knocking the wind out of him.
4. [uncountable + singular or plural verb, also **winds**(plural)] the group of musical instruments in an orchestra that produce sounds when you blow into them, especially woodwind instruments; the musicians who play those instruments
   - The wind plays/play beautifully in thes passage.

## [woodwind(noun, uncountable + singular or plural verb, also **woodwinds**[plural])](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/woodwind)

1. the group of musical instruments in an orchestra that are traditionally made of wood and are played by blowing. Flutes, clarinets and bassoons are all woodwind instruments.
   - The woodwind plays/play beautifully in this passage.

## [wind(verb)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/wind1_2)

> Verb Forms: wind / winds / winded / winded / winding

1. [usually passive] to make somebody unable to breathe easily for a short time
   - **be winded (by something)** He was momentarily winded by the blow to his stomach.
2. **wind somebody** (British English) to gently hit or rub a baby's back to make it burp(= release gas from its stomach through its mouth); SYNONYM: burp

## [wind(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/wind2_1)

1. an act of turning, wrapping or twisting something around itself or in a circle
   - Give the handle another couple of winds.

## [wind(verb)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/wind2_2)

> Verb Forms: wind / winds / wound / wound / winding

1. (of a road, river, etc.) to have many bends and twists
   - A wooden staircase winds up to the attic.
2. [transitive] **wind something + adv./prep.** to wrap or twist something around itself or something else
   - They wind individual strands of fibre together to make home-made rope
3. to operate a tape, film, etc. so that it moves nearer to its ending or starting position
   - **wind something (on)** With these cameras, after you click the shutter, you have to wind the film on.
4. to make a clock or machine work by turning a knob, handle, etc. several times; to be able to be made to work in this way
   - **wind up** It was one of those old-fashioned gramophones that winds up.
5. [transitive] **wind something** to turn a handle several times
   - You operate the trapdoor by winding this handle.

## [the Windmill Theatre](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/the-windmill-theatre)

1. a former small theatre in the West End of London(1931-1964). It was well known for its shows of dancing by naked or almost naked women. Comedians used to tell jokes between the dancing shows, and many famous performers (including Tony Hancock and Peter Sellers) started their careers there. It was the only theatre that stayed open when bombs were being dropeed on London during the Second World War.

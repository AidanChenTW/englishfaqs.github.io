<h1 style="text-align: center;">SUBJECT</h1>

## [subject(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/subject_1)
- [of conversation/book] a thing or person that is being discussed, described or dealt with
- [at school/college] an area of knowledge studied in a school, college, etc.
- [of picture/photograph] a person or thing that is the main feature of a picture or photograph, or that a work of art is based on
- [of experiment] a person or thing being used to study something, especially in an experiment
- [grammar] a noun, noun phrase or pronoun representing the person or thing that performs the action of the verb, about which something is stated or, in a passive sentence, that is affected by the action of the verb
- [of country] a person who belongs to a particular country, especially one with a king or queen

## [subject(adj)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/subject_2)
> formal
- likely to be affected by something, especially something bad
- depending on something in order to be completed or agreed
- under the authority of something/somebody
- [only before noun] controlled by the government of another country

## [subject(verb)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/subject_3)
> formal
> Verb Forms: subject / subjects / subjected / subjected / subjecting
- to bring a country or group of people under your control, especially by using force

## [subjection(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/subjection)
> formal
- the process of bringing a country or group of people under your control, especially by using force; the situation of being controlled in this way

## [subjective(adj)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/subjective)
- based on your own ideas or opinions rather than facts and therefore sometimes unfair
- existing in somebody's mind rather that in the outside world
- (grammar) the subjective case is the one which is used for the subject of a sentence

## [subjectively(adv)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/subjectively)
- in a way that is based on a person's own ideas, opinions or feelings rather than the facts
- in a way that is based on what is in somebody's mind rather than on facts that can be proved

## [subjectivism(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/subjectivism)
> philosophy
- the theory that all knowledge and moral values are subjective rather than based on truth that actually exists in the real world

## [subjectivity(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/subjectivity)
- the fact of being influenced by personal ideas, opinions or feelings, rather than facts
- the fact of existing in somebody's mind rather than the outside world

## [subject-line(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/subject-line)
- the line of text at the start of an email that says what it is about; the space where this is written

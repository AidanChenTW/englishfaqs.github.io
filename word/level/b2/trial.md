## [trial(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/trial_1)

1. a formal examiniation of evidence in court by a judge and often a jury, to decide if somebody accused of a crime is guilty or not
   - She will stand trial/go on trial for fraud.
2. the process of testing the ability, quality or performance of somebody/something, especially before you make a final decision about them
   - Australia and the US have conducted joint trials of the drone.
3. [countable, usually plural] (North American English also tryout) a competition or series of tests to find the best players for a sports team or an important event
   - Olympic trials
4. [countable, usually plural] an event at which animals compete or perform
   - horse trials
5. [countable] an experience or a person that causes difficulties for somebody
   - the trials and tribulations of married life
6. [trial and error(idiom)] the process of solving a problem by trying various methods until you find a method that is successful
   - Children learn to use computer programs by trial and error.

## [trial(verb)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/trial_2)

> (British English), Verb Forms: trial / trials; (North American English) trialed / trialled; (North American English) trialed / trialling; (North American English) trialing

1. **trial (something)** to test the ability, quality or performance of something to see if it will be effective or successful

## [trial balloon(noun, especially North American English)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/trial-balloon)

1. something that you say or do to find out what people think about a course of action before you take it
   - They may be floating a trial ballon to test reaction in the region.

## [trial run(noun, also **test run**)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/trial-run)

1. a test of how well something new works, so that you can see if any changes are necessary
   - This year is a trial run for the new service, before it is officially introduced in 2011.

## [trials riding(noun, uncountable)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/trials-riding)

> also **trials biking**  
> also **observed trials** [uncountable + singular or plural verb]

1. the sport of riding a specially designed motorcycle or bicycle through a course that has objects on the ground, which is designed to test a rider's ability

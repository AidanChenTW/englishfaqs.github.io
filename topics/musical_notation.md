### Musical Notation 音樂記譜法(系統)

### [note(noun)(B2) 音符](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/note_1)

- [countable] a single sound of a particular length and pitch(= how high or low a sound is), made by the voice or a musical instrument; the written or printed sign for a musical note
  - He played the first few notes of the tune.
  - high/low notes
  - I played a lot of wrong notes because I was so nervous

### [rest(noun)(C2) 休止符](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/rest_1)

- [notable, uncountable] a period of silence between notes; a sign that shows a rest between notes

### [clef 譜號](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/clef)

- a symbol at the beginning of a line of printed music (called a stave or staff) that shows the pitch of the notes on it

### [tie 延音線(連接兩個相同音高的音符)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/tie_2)

- a curved line written over two notes of the same pitch (= how high or low a note is) to show that they are able to be played or sung as one note

### [time signature 拍號(如 4/4 拍)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/time-signature)

- a sign at the start of a piece of music, usually in the form of numbers, showing the number of beats (= units of rhythm) in each bar

### Bar / Measure 小節

- [bar(British English)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/bar_1)
- [measure(North American English)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/measure_2)
  - one of the short sections of equal length that a piece of music is divided into, and the notes that are in it
    - four beats to the bar
    - the opening bars of a piece of music
    - She played a few bars on the piano.
    - The band began playing the opening measures.

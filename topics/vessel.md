## [vessel(noun)(C1)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/vessel)

1. a tube that carries blood through the body of a person or an animal, or liquid through the parts of a plant
   * to burst/rupture a blood vessel
2. (formal) a ship or large boat(正式/法律/專業術語。泛指所有水上交通工具)
   * ocean-going vessels
   * a small fishing vessel
3. (old use or specialist) a container used for holding liquids, such as a bowl, cup, etc.
   * a Bronze Age drinking vessel
   * ancient bronze ritual vessels

## [ship(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/ship_1)

1. a large boat that carries people or goods by sea
   * The yard builds ships for the Navy.
   * **on a ship** The others sailed on a later ship.
   * **aboard a ship** The goods had been loaded aboard a ship destined for Montreal.
   * There are two restaurants on board ship.
   * a cargo/cruise/merchant ship.
   * a Navy ship
   * a ship's company (= the crew of a ship)
   * a ship's captain/crew/officers
   * **by ship** Raw materials and labour come by ship, rail or road.
   * The captain gave the order to abandon ship (= to leave the ship because it was sinking).
   * a fleet of ships.
   * They boarded a ship bound for India.
   * When the ship docked at Southampton he was rushed to hospital.

## [boad(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/boat)

1. a vehicle(smaller than a ship) that travels on water, moved by oars, sails or a motor
   * a rowing/sailing boat
   * a fishing boat
   * You can take a boat trip along the coast.
   * The country intends to build a special fleet of patrol boats.
   * a rescue/pleasure/passenger boat
   * The boat capsized and sank
2. any ship
   * 'How are you going to Franch?''We're going by boat(=by ferry).'
   * The island is just a short boat ride from the mainland.

## [yacht(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/yacht)

1. (North American English also **sailboat**) a sailing boat, often also with an engine and a place to sleep on board, used for pleasure trips and racing
   * a 12-metre racing yacht
   * a yacht club/race
2. a large boat with an engine and a place to sleep on board, used for pleasure trips
   * a luxury yacht
   * a motor yacht
  
## [ferry(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/ferry_1)

> plural ferries

1. a boat or ship that carries people, vehicles and goods across a river aor across a narrow part of the sea
   * a passenger/car ferry
   * the Staten Island ferry
   * the ferry terminal at Calais
   * the Dover-Calais ferry crossing
   * the cross-channel ferry service
   * to take/catch the ferry
   * We boarded the ferry at Ostend

## [canoe(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/canoe_1)

1. a light narrow boat that you move along in the water with a paddle
   * We crossed the lake by canoe.

## [kayak(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/kayak)

1. a light canoe in which the part where you sit is covered over

## [tug(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/tug_2)

1. (also tugboat) a small powerful boat for pulling ships, especially into a harbour or up a river
2. a sudden, hard pull
   * I felt a tug at my sleeve.
   * She gave her sister's hair a sharp tug.

## [barge(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/barge_1)

1. a large boat with a flat bottom, used for carrying goods and people on canals and rivers
   * They travel by barge.
   * We spent the summber cruising the canals of France in a barge.

## [submarine(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/submarine_1)

> also informal **sub**

1. a ship that can travel underwater
   * a nuclear submarine
   * a submarine base
   * submarines carrying nuclear warheads
   * the problem of submarines operating too close to fishing vessels
2. (also **submarine sandwich, hero**)(all North American English) a long bread roll split open along its length and filed with various types of food

## [raft(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/raft)

1. a flat structure made of pieces of wood tied together and used as a boat or floating platform
2. a small boat made of rubber or plastic that is filled with air
   * an inflatable raft
   * The two airmen inflated the life raft.
3. [usually singular] **raft of something** (informal) a large number or amount of something
   * a whole raft of new proposals

## [carrier(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/carrier)

1. a company that carries goods or passengers from one place to another, especially by air
   * Delta Air Lines, the third-largest carrier in the US
2. a military vehicle or ship that carries soldiers or equipment from one place to another
3. a person or animal that passes a disease to other people or animals, usually without showing any symptoms
4. a metal frame that is fixed to a bicycle and used for carrying bags
5. a person or thing that carries something
   * Aquarius, the Water Carrier
   * a baby carrier(= for carrying a baby on your back or in fron of you)
6. (also **carrier bag**(both British English), North American English **shopping bag**) a paper or plastic bag for carrying shopping
7. a company that provides a phone or internet service
   * a telecoms carrier
   * mobile-phone carriers

## [aircraft carrier(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/aircraft-carrier)

* a large ship that carries aircraft that use it as a base to land on and take off from

## [tanker(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/tanker)

* a ship or lorry that carries oil, gas or petrol in large quantities
  * an oil tanker

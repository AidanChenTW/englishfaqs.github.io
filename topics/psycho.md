## PSYCHO-

### [psycho(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psycho_1)

> plural **psychos**, informal, offensive

1. an offensive word for a person who you think is crazy, strange or violent

### [psycho(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psycho_2)

> informal, often offensive

1. crazy, strange or violent; It is offensive to use this word to describe somebody with a mental illness.
   - They said he had 'gone psycho'.

### [Psycho](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psycho_3)

> a US film(1960) directed by Alfred Hitchcock. Anthony Perkins played Norman Bates, a man suffering from a mental illness who owns a hotel and kills people who stay there. The scene in which he kills a woman in a shower is considered one of the most frightening ever filmed. Perkins later played Bates in three more films.

### [psycho-(combining form)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psycho_4)

> also **psych-**; in nouns, adjectives and adverbs

1. connected with the mind
   - psychology
   - psychiatric

### [psychoactive(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychoactive)

> specialist

1. (of a drug) affecting the mind

### [psychoanalyse(verb)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychoanalyse)

> British English; North American English **-yze**; also **analyse**, **analyze**  
> Verb Forms: psychoanalyse / psychoanalyses / psychoanalysed / psychoanalysed / psychoanalysing

1. **psychoanalyse somebody** to treat or study somebody using psychoanalysis

### [psychoanalysis(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychoanalysis)

> also **analysis**; uncountable

1. a method of treating somebody who has problems with their mental health by encouraging them to talk about past experiences and feelings, and trying to help them understand fears and feelings that they were not conscious of

### [psychoanalyst(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychoanalyst)

> also **analyst**

1. a person who treats patients using psychoanalysis

### [psychoanalytic(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychoanalytic)

> anly before noun

1. using or connected with a method of treating somebody who has problems with their mental health by encouraging them to talk about past experiences and feelings, and trying to help them understand fears and feelings that they were not conscious of
   - a psychoanalytic approach

### [psychoanalytically(adverb)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychoanalytically)

1. in a way that uses or relates to psychoanalysis (= treatment of mental health problems by encouraging somebody to talk about past experiences and feelings, in order to understand fears and feelings that they were not aware of)

### [psychobabble(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychobabble)

> uncountable; informal; disapproving

1. the language that people use when they talk about feelings and emotional problems, that sounds very scientific, but really has little meaning

### [psychodrama(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychodrama)

1. a form of psychotherapy (= treatment for mental health problems that involves talking with a trained person) in which people act events from their past to help them understand their feelings
2. a play or film in which the minds and feelings of the characters are more important than the events

### [psychokinesis(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychokinesis)

> uncountable

1. the act of moving an object by using the power of the mind

### [psycholinguistic(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psycholinguistic)

1. connected with the study of how the mind processes and produces language
   - psycholiguistic experiments

### [psycholinguistics(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psycholinguistics)

> uncountable

1. the study of how the mind processes and produces language

### [psychoological(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychological)

1. [usually before noun] connected with a person's mind and the way in which it works
   - a psychological novel (= one that examines the minds of the characters)
2. [only before noun] connected with the study of psychology
   - a psychological study of refugee children came up with some worrying results
3. **[idioms] the psychological moment**: the best time to do something in order for it to be successful
   - The trick is in finding the (right) psychological moment to ask him for more money.

### [psychologically(adverb)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychologically)

1. in a way that is connected with a person's mind and the way in which it works
   - Psychologically, the defeat was devastating.
2. in a way that is connected with the study of psychology
   - Psychologically speaking, 'closure' is an illusion.

### [psychological warfare(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychological-warfare)

> uncountable

1. things that are said and done in order to make an opponent believe that they cannot win a war, a competition, etc.
   - a subtle form of psychological warfare

### [psychologist(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychologist)

1. a scientist who studies and is trained in psychology
   - I made an appointment with the school psychologist.

### [psychology(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychology)

1. [uncountable] the scientific study of the mind and how it influences behaviour
   - She's a professor of psychology at Harvard University.
2. [singular] the kind of mind that somebody has that makes them think or behave in a particular way
   - Watching the shoppers at the sales gave her a first-hand insight into crowd psychology.
3. [singular] how the mind influences behaviour in a particular area of life
   -**psychology of something** the psychology of interpersonal relationships

### [psychometric(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychometric)

> only before noun, specialist

1. used for measuring mental abilities and processes
   - psychometric testing

### [psychopath(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychopath)

1. (psychology) a person who has a serious personality disorder that means they do not care about other people's feelings, do not feel sorry when they have done something bad, and may want to be violent or cruel towards others
2. (informal, offensive) an offensive word for a person who you think is strange and violent

### [psychopathic(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychopathic)

> psychology

1. suffering from or caused by a serious personality disorder that means somebody does not care about other people's feelings, does not feel sorry when they have done something bad, and may want to be violent or cruel towards others
   - a psychopathic killer/disorder

### [psychopathology(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychopathology)

1. [uncountable] the scientific study of mental illness
2. [countable] an illness or condition that affects somebody's mind or their behaviour

### [psychopathy(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychopathy)

> uncountable; psychology

1. a serious personality disorder that means somebody does not care about other people's feelings, does not feel sorry when they have done something bad, and many want to be violent or cruel towards others

### [psychosexual(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychosexual)

1. involving the psychological aspects of sexual desire

### [psychosis(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychosis)

> plural **psychoses**

1. a serious mental health condition in which somebody sees or hears things that are not there, or believes things that are not there, or believes things that are not true

### [psychosomatic(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychosomatic)

1. (of an illness) caused by stress and worry, rather than by a physical problem such as an infection
   - I began to experience psychosomatic symptoms such as stomach aches and headaches
2. (specialist) connected with the relationship between the mind and the body

### [psychotherapist(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychotherapist)

> also **therapist**

1. a person who is trained to treat somebody who has problems with their mental health by talking with them

### [psychotherapy(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychotherapy)

> also **therapy**; uncountable

1. the treatment of mental health problems by talking with a therapist (= a person trained to do this)

### [psychotic(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychotic_1)

> psychology

1. affected or caused by a serious mental illness, in which somebody sees or hears things that are not there, or believes things that are not true
   - He suffered a psychotic episode two years ago.

### [psychotic(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychotic_2)

> psychology

1. a person who has psychosis (= a serious mental illness in which somebody sees or hears things that are not there, or believes things that are not true)

### [psychotropic(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychotropic)

> usually before noun; medical

1. relating to drugs or substances that affect a person's mental state
   - psychotropic medication/drugs

### [American Psycho](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/american-psycho)

1. a novel (1991) by Bret Easton Ellis about a young man living in Manhattan who is a psychopath (= a person with a severe personality disorder that means they do not care about other people's feelings and may want to be violent or cruel towards them). The story was made into a popular film in 2000.

## PSYCH-

### [psych out(phrasal verb)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psych-out)

> **psych somebody out (of something)**

1. (informal) to make an opponent feel less confident by saying or doing things that make you seem better, stronger, etc. that them

### [psych up(phrasal verb)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psych-up)

> **psych somebody/yourself up (for something)**

1. (informal) to prepare somebody/yourself mentally for something difficult or unpleasant
   - I'd got myself all psyched up for the interview and then it was called off at the last minute.

### [psyche(noun, formal)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psyche)

1. the mind; your deepest feelings and attitudes
   - In his 1889 treatise the sociologist examines the effect that wealth has on the human psyche.

### [psychedelia(noun, uncountable)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychedelia)

1. music, art, fashion, etc. that is created as a result of the effects of psychedelic drugs

### [psychedelic(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychedelic)

> usually before noun

1. (of drugs) causing the user to see and hear things that are not there or that do not exist; to hallucinate
2. (of art, music, clothes, etc.) having bright colours, strange sounds, etc. like those that are exeprienced when taking psychedelic drugs

### [psychiatric(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychiatric)

1. relating to psychiatry or to mental illnesses
   - a psychiatric hospital/nurse/patient

### [psychiatrist(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychiatrist)

1. a doctor who studies and treats mental illnesses
   - the secrets revealed on the psychiatrist's couch

### [psychiatry(noun, uncountable)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychiatry)

1. the study and treatment of mental illnesses

### [psychic(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychic_1)

1. (also less frequent **psychical**) connected with strange powers of the mind and not able to be explained by natural laws; SYNONYM: paranormal
   - psychic energy/forces/phenomena/powers
2. (of a person) seeming to have strange mental powers and to be able to do things that are not possible according to natural laws
   - How am I supposed to know - I'm not psychic!
3. (also less frequent **psychical**) (formal) connected with the mind rather than the body

### [psychic(noun)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychic_2)

1. a person who claims to have strange mental powers so that they can do things that are not possible according to natural laws, such as predicting the future and speaking to dead people

### [psychically(adverb)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psychically)

1. in a way that is connected with strange powers of the mind that cannot be explained by natural laws
   - In the story, the boy and the dolphin are psychically linked, so they know when each other is nearby.
2. in a way that uses strange mental powers to be able to do things that are not possible according to natural laws
   - There is a claim that some people can psychically influence computers.
3. (formal) in a way that is connected with the mind rather than the body
   - psychically damaged young men and women

### [psyched(adjective)](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/psyched)

> not before noun; especially North American English, informal

1. excited, especially about something that is going to happen
   - The kids are really psyched about the vacation
